Backpacking in Lumbini


This Unesco World heritage site is famous for being the birthplace of Buddha. There are about 25 Buddhist monasteries scattered around Lumbini. Almost every Buddhist country in the world has donated their own temple in Lumbini. It’s a great way to see the different styles of architecture from each country. The most famous one is the Mayadevi Temple, according to historians and archaeologist this is the site where Buddha is born. To be fair, a lot of people get disappointed in Lumbini because they aren't that interested in Buddhism (or become temple sick). Only pay a visit to Lumbini if you’re interested in Buddhism and want to go on a spiritual adventure.

Things to do


Maya Devi Temple

The most famous landmark in Lumbini and an iconic temple for Buddhism. According to Buddhist this is the temple where Buddha set his first steps. On the site a sandstone marks the exact spot where he is born. Pilgrims from all over the world come to Lumbini to visit this temple.




Royal Thai Buddhist Monastery

One of the biggest monasteries in Lumbini. The temple is made out of white marble and it’s a great area to roam around. Worth a visit if you’ve never seen a Thai style monastery.





Myanmar Golden Temple

Ever been to Myanmar? You will straight away recognise this temple style. The temple stands out from the rest because of its yellow (golden) colour. Arguably one of the most beautiful temples in Lumbini.





Where to stay


Lumbini can easily be covered in one day. So usually you only want to stay here for 1 or 2 nights. Near the northern entrance you can find the more expensive accommodations. The cheaper ones are at the east entrance. There isn’t a lot of choice in Lumbini so it can pay off to book in advance to get a descent accommodation.


  • Lumbini Garden Lodge $$$ | Probably the best budget option in Lumbini. Typical basic Nepali hotel rooms but here they’ve hot showers. Perfectly located near the development zone. Most people that stay here are backpackers.

  • Mirage Inn $$$ | Modern hotel with spacious rooms. Located near gate 5 of the development zone which is the closest one to the Maya Devi Temple. Perfect if you want to have a comfortable stay in Lumbini.


Food


There aren’t a lot of (good) options in Lumbini. Some hotels offer descent food but eating at one of the handful restaurants is usually a better idea.


  • Lumbini Invitation 365 Restaurant $$$ | Indian & Nepalese | According to most people this is the best place to eat in Lumbini. It’s a good mix of Indian and Nepalese dishes. Great value for money.

  • GG Cafe & Restaurant $$$ | Nepalese, Indian & Thai | Not the greatest food in Lumbini but staff and ambience is pretty good. They’ve a couple Thai dishes on the menu if you’re in for a change.

Budget Tips


I don’t have a lot of tips to save money in Lumbini. There isn’t a lot to do where you can save money on. The cheapest accommodation is around 5 USD a night. The entrance fee for Lumbini Development site is 200 NPR and 50 NPR for the museum.


Getting around


Lumbini is a small town which mostly can be covered by foot. Otherwise it’s possible to rent a bike or an electric rickshaw.

Accommodations can arrange bus tickets to Kathmandu. The journey takes 8-10 hours depending on the amount of stops and traffic. The cheapest way is the public bus that leaves every day from the Lumbini Bazaar (around 550 NPR). A different option is to take a tourist bus with aircon, this will be around 700 – 850 NPR.
