Backpacking in Pokhara


Pokhara is the place in Nepal that ticks all the boxes. You can unwind at the lake or seek adrenaline and paraglide above the lake. If you’re done with that you can start explore the best hikes in the world. Pokhara is the gateway to the Annapurna region and from here you can start your journey. Pokhara has a lot to offer and you can’t miss it when you’re backpacking in Nepal.

Things to do


Phewa Lake

The lake is very peaceful and great to explore. You can hire a bike and cycle around the lake but it’s also possible to rent a kayak. At the Lakeside in Pokhara you can find hotels, restaurants and bars catering to the tourists. Despite the Lakeside is very touristy it’s still possible to watch the locals living their life. The lake is one of the most memorable sites in Nepal.



Paragliding

Paragliders from all over the world come to Pokhara to enjoy their hobby. The view over the lake is stunning and the best way to enjoy it is gliding in the air. If you’ve good circumstances it’s possible to glide longer than 30 minutes in the air. After your flight you will land near the bank of the lake. It’s an unforgettable experience and highly recommended when you’re in Pokhara.



World Peace Pagoda

Also called the Shanti Stupa. The hike to this Buddhist pagoda is a perfect preparation for a hike in the Annapurna region or just a great activity to spend your day. The most entertaining way to get there is by boat and hike to the top. From the Pagoda you’ve an amazing view over the Annapurna range. Not eager to hike? It’s also possible to get here by car or bus.



Sarangkot

West of Pokhara is the small village Sarangkot. It’s famous for its extraordinary views over the Himalaya. Especially during sunsets and sunrises this will be a breathtaking experience. It’s possible to stay overnight in Sarangkot. Bring your camera and photograph one of the best panoramas the world has to offer.



Where to stay


The best area to stay in Pokhara is Lakeside. Don’t even consider other areas because the rest of Pokhara is pretty hectic and dirty. Lakeside is a big contrast to the rest of the city, it’s clean, peaceful and you can find here the best accommodation. There aren't a lot of hostels in Pokhara. Most types of accomodation are guesthouses. A couple good places to stay in Lakeside are:


  • Forest Lake Backpackers Hostel $$$ | One of the few real hostels in Pokhara. Really comfortable cabin beds. It's located next to the lake and has great views from the rooftop.

  • Hotel Eagle Zone $$$ | One of the hotels with great views over the lake. Fan of a good breakfast? This is your place to stay. Staff is friendly and helpfull.

  • The Mountain House $$$ | Guesthouse runned by a very helpful family. Spacious rooms for a great price.

Food


Lakeside is covered with great restaurants. There’re a lot of different nationalities in Pokhara so you can find all cuisines of the world here. They’re so many restaurants that it’s hard to make a selection. This are 3 places we really enjoyed during our stay.


  • Frituur No.1 $$$ | Belgium fries & beers | Want something else than Dal Bhat? This place has great Belgium fries and delicious hamburgers. And of course the best collection of Belgium Beers.

  • Fresh Elements $$$ | International | Breakfast, lunch or dinner? You can’t go wrong at Fresh Elements. It’s has a beautiful setup with a great atmosphere.

  • Little Windows Cafe $$$ | International | Delicious food for a great price. Also one of the places with the best Wi-Fi in Pokhara. The sizzling fajitas are very popular.

Budget Tips


Backpacking in Nepal is really cheap. But still it would be nice to save some money. Down below you can find a couple tips to save money in Pokhara. Want to know how much budget you need for backpacking in Nepal? Go visit our guide about the [budget you need for Nepal].


  • If you’re planning to paraglide in Pokhara it’s wise to ask around for prices. The price (included with video and photos) will be around $100. Most agencies and guesthouses will offer you this price but some will charge you more.

  • One of the cheapest ways to explore the Phewa Lake is by bicycle. For around $5 per day you can rent a bike and cycle around the lake. It’s a good way to escape the tourists and see the locals living their daily life.

Getting around


Once you're in Lakeside the easiest way to get around is by foot. But to get there can sometimes be a bit of a challenge. If you're coming from the airport or the bus stand the best way to go to Lakeside is by taxi. If you want to explore the lake the best way to get around is by bike.


Getting around by Bike

Everywhere in Lakeside you can find bicycle rental places. There's a lot of difference in quality between them so check your bikes first. Be prepared to have a sore buttocks for a couple days if you get a shitty bike. Usually it will cost you about $5 per day.


Getting around by Taxi

If you arrive by bus in Pokhara you will be harassed by loads of taxi drivers. Don’t go with the first one that approached you but ask around. Make clear where you want to go otherwise they take you to their ‘recommended’ hotel. From the Pokhara bus stand to Lakeside you will pay between Rs 300 – 400.


Duration from Lakeside to popular destinations:

  • Lakeside Pokhara Tourist bus stand 10 min
  • Lakeside Pokhara Pokhara Baglung Bus Park10 min
  • Lakeside Pokhara World Peace Pagoda 30 min
  • Lakeside Pokhara Sarangkot 30 min


Getting around by bus

It's possible to take local busses from Lakeside to the bus stands and the airport. It isn't really recommended though. It's a confusing and frustrating experience. But if you want to save money this will be your choice of transport. A ticket will cost you Rs 15.
