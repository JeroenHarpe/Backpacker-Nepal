Backpacking in Kathmandu


People who visit Kathmandu are usually divided in two camps, 1 camp thinks it’s hectic and crowded and the other camps think that it’s a very nice and relaxed place. I’m in the latter camp, I agree there’s a lot of traffic and people but compared to Indian cities it’s peaceful. Anyway, there's a very big change you start your Nepalese journey in Kathmandu. It can be a bit daunting at first but eventually you will find your way here. To make the most of your time in Nepal read our tips below.

Things to do


Durbar Square

Kathmandu’s most famous square with beautiful historic sights. Most of the sights got heavily damaged in the 2015 earthquake but it’s still worth a visit. A lot of backpackers go here to spot the Raj Kumari. A young girl chosen through an ancient selection process to become the human incarnation of the Hindu mother goddess called Durga. It’s a weird experience but check it out when you go to Durbar Square.



Thamel

If you’re going to stay a couple days in Kathmandu you most definitely going to stay in Thamel. It’s the area where all backpackers gather. Typically known for its narrow alleys with lots of shops, restaurants, cafés and surprisingly good night life. Go here to organize your Nepalese adventures or just to enjoy the backpack scene in Kathmandu.



Garden of Dreams

Want to escape the crowdedness of Kathmandu? Go visit the Garden of Dreams, a 6,895 square metres garden built in 1920. This is the place where you can find young Nepalese couples going on a date or tourists who peacefully want to read their book.




Swayambhunath Temple

This is the temple that you see on a lot of covers. It’s one of the most famous sites in Kathmandu and that’s for a reason. From the temple you’ve a great view over Kathmandu. Before you can enjoy the view you’ve to go over a long stairway. When you go up, watch out for cheeky monkeys that try to steal your food!



Pashupatinath Temple

This temple makes you feel like you’re in India. The Hindu Temple is located on the banks of the Bagmati River. It’s an intriguing site where you can easily spend more time than you planned. It’s a nice change to the most Buddhist Temples in Kathmandu.



Where to stay


The best area to stay as a backpacker in Kathmandu is Thamel. In this area you will find a lot of hostels, hotels, restaurants and shops. With its narrow alleys and hippie vibes it’s a great place to meet other travelers or locals. Most of the hostels have a really relaxed vibe and great facilities. If you’re looking for a place to stay consider one of these options:


  • Zostel Kathmandu $$$ | A very popular chain from India that now has hostels in Nepal. It's a bit more expensive but the hostel has great facilities.

  • Alobar1000 $$$ | Kathmandu's most famous hostel. If you're looking to party or to relax you can have it both in this hostel. Great common areas.

  • Thamel Ruyi $$$ | This hotel is great value for your money. They've bunkbeds and rooms for a great budget. The rooftop has a great view as well.

Food


There are great options for food in Kathmandu and it's possible to find almost every cuisine. For the best restaurants and cafés you’ve to be in Thamel. We selected a couple options if you’re out of ideas where to eat.


  • Thamel Doner Kebab $$$ | Middle Eastern | Almost every backpacker has been here to eat a great falaffel wrap. It's cheap and perfect if you're in a hurry. It's located in the centre of Thamel.

  • Nirvana Cafe & Restaurant $$$ | Nepalese & Western | If you're going to the Swayambhunath Temple (Monkey Temple) try to go here as well. It's about a 10 min walk and it has a great view and delicious Momos. Great to have lunch after a visit to the temple.

  • Forest and Plate $$$ | European, Asian, Nepalese | When you want to celebrate a completion of a hike this is a good place to celebrate it. You can find it on the 3rd floor of the Sagarmartha complex in Thamel.

  • Fire and Ice Pizza $$$ | Pizza | The best pizza's in Kathmandu. The place is actually owned by an Italian woman and you can taste it. Take into account that it can get really busy here.

Budget Tips


Backpacking in Nepal is really cheap. But still it would be nice to save some money. Down below you can find a couple tips to save money in Kathmandu. Want to know how much budget you need for backpacking in Nepal? Go visit our guide about the [budget you need for Nepal].


  • Want to save money on food but still want a delicious meal? Go to Thamel Doner Kebab (as mentioned above). For just 200 NPR you can have a delicious Falaffel Wrap.

  • When you’re in Kathmandu and planning to do a multiday hike try to buy as much as possible in Thamel. Everywhere you can find stores to buy clothes you will need. In most stores it’s also possible to rent stuff, for example a sleeping bag is useful to rent instead of buying. Visit our guides about [stuff you need for a hike] or [preparing for a hike in Nepal] for more information.

  • If you want to go somewhere in the Kathmandu Valley on a budget go to Ratna Park Bus station. These local busses are quite an experience and a good way to save money compared to the more expensive tourist busses. See more information down below at the ‘getting around’ section.

Getting around


There are many options to get around in Kathmandu. It’s even possible to take a bike if you want to but with the heavy traffic it’s not recommended. The fastest way (and still can take a while) is by taxi. The cheapest way are the local busses.


Getting around by Taxi

The taxis in Kathmandu are not your average taxis, they’re extremely small and only fit about 2 people with 2 bags. Keep this in mind because it can cause a lot of inconvenience when you’re in a hurry. The taxis don’t have a meter. Even it’s not allowed by law but in general you’ve to bargain for your ride. In general a 15 min ride would cost you 300 NPR but it can varies a lot.


Duration from Thamel to popular destinations:

  • Thamel Swayambhunath Temple 15 min
  • Thamel Durbar Square 10 min
  • Thamel Bhaktapur 40 min
  • Thamel Patan 25 min


Getting around by bus

Traveling by bus around Kathmandu is an experience. Definitely don’t go with the bus when you’re in a hurry. It can be very confusing and frustrating at times but if you want to connect with locals this is a good option for you. Usually the local busses don’t have signs with the destinations on them. So ask around for the bus to your destination. Once you’re in the right bus you pay to the conductor. In the local busses this is usually a young bloke who tries to lure more people in the bus. Because of this your journey can take a bit lounger but hey, it’s part of the experience.

Tourist busses are a lot more comfortable and straight forward. Although there’s a big difference in quality between different bus companies. Just go to the bus station of your destination and check the different bus companies. Usually they’re a couple busses waiting where you can choose from.

Down below you can find the bus stations for most destinations:

  • Ratna Park Bus Station Kathmandu Valley (Patan & Dhulikhel)
  • Gongabu Bus Station Lumbini & Pokhara
  • Tourist bus stand Chitwan & Pokhara
  • Pasang Lhamo Transport stand Langtang Region
